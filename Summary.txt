Executive Business Report

Sales Forecasting & Demand Intelligence System

Executive Summary

This project analyzes historical retail sales data to understand
business performance, identify seasonal trends, forecast future demand,
detect unusual sales patterns, and segment products based on demand
characteristics. The analysis combines exploratory data analysis,
time-series forecasting, anomaly detection, and machine learning
techniques to support better inventory planning and business
decision-making.

The dataset covers sales transactions across multiple regions, product
categories, and sub-categories. Throughout the analysis, clear upward
sales trends, recurring seasonal patterns, and differences in regional
and category performance were observed. Forecasting models were
evaluated using MAE, RMSE, and MAPE, while anomaly detection highlighted
exceptional sales events that deserve further business investigation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Business Performance Analysis

Overall Sales Trend

The monthly sales trend shows that business performance generally
improved over time. Although individual months experienced fluctuations,
the long-term trend continued to increase, indicating healthy business
growth. Sales reached their highest levels during the later part of the
dataset, suggesting improved customer demand and stronger market
performance.

The time-series decomposition further confirmed three important
characteristics:

• A positive long-term trend indicating business growth. • Strong
seasonal behaviour where similar sales patterns repeat every year. •
Random residual variations caused by promotions, customer demand
changes, and external business factors.

The Augmented Dickey-Fuller (ADF) test confirmed that the sales series
is stationary after differencing, making it suitable for forecasting
models.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Category Analysis

Technology generated the highest total sales among all product
categories, making it the strongest revenue contributor. Furniture also
maintained consistent sales performance throughout the period, while
Office Supplies demonstrated stable demand with moderate fluctuations.

Monthly category analysis showed that all three categories experienced
seasonal peaks during high-demand periods, especially towards the end of
each year. These seasonal increases indicate opportunities for targeted
inventory planning and promotional campaigns.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Regional Analysis

The West region recorded the highest overall sales, followed closely by
the East region. Central and South regions generated comparatively lower
revenue, indicating potential opportunities for market expansion or
regional marketing initiatives.

Average shipping time remained close to four days across all regions,
suggesting that logistics performance was relatively consistent
regardless of location.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Forecasting Results

Three forecasting techniques were evaluated:

• SARIMA • Prophet • XGBoost

The model comparison produced the following results:

XGBoost MAE : 14868.07 RMSE : 18606.31 MAPE : 14.56%

SARIMA MAE : 18031.40 RMSE : 19009.18 MAPE : 18.97%

Prophet MAE : 40970.33 RMSE : 53868.95 MAPE : 40.04%

Among all models, XGBoost achieved the lowest forecasting error, making
it the recommended model for future sales prediction. SARIMA also
performed reasonably well, whereas Prophet produced comparatively larger
prediction errors for this dataset.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Anomaly Detection

Isolation Forest successfully identified unusual weekly sales
observations. Several significant spikes and drops were detected
throughout the dataset, particularly during promotional periods and the
final months of the observation period.

These anomalies may represent:

• Festival or holiday sales • Marketing campaigns • Inventory shortages
• Exceptional customer demand • Operational disruptions

Monitoring these events can help management understand unusual business
behaviour and improve future planning.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Product Demand Segmentation

K-Means clustering grouped product sub-categories into four demand
segments:

• High Volume Stable • Growing Demand • Low Volume • Declining

Products such as Copiers and Machines were identified as high-value
items, while several other sub-categories belonged to growing or stable
demand groups. This segmentation provides valuable guidance for
inventory optimization.

Recommended actions include:

• Maintain higher inventory for high-volume products. • Increase stock
gradually for growing-demand products. • Closely monitor low-volume
items. • Reduce inventory for declining products to minimize holding
costs.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Business Recommendations

1.  Deploy the XGBoost forecasting model for operational demand
    forecasting.
2.  Prioritize inventory planning for Technology products and the West
    region.
3.  Investigate detected anomalies to understand promotional and
    seasonal impacts.
4.  Use product clusters to optimize warehouse storage and replenishment
    policies.
5.  Continue monitoring seasonal trends to prepare inventory before peak
    demand periods.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

Conclusion

The project successfully integrates data analysis, forecasting, anomaly
detection, and machine learning into a unified business intelligence
solution. The results indicate sustained business growth, clear seasonal
demand patterns, and opportunities for improved inventory planning.

By implementing the XGBoost forecasting model together with anomaly
monitoring and product segmentation, the organization can improve
forecasting accuracy, reduce inventory costs, and make more informed
strategic decisions.
